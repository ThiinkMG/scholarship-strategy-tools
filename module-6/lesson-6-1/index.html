Lesson 6.1 — Financial Aid Appeals

Appeal Outline
Builder

Build a structured financial aid appeal letter step by step. Answer guided prompts on the left and watch your appeal letter come together in real time on the right.

Appeal Completeness0%
👤
Your Information
Basic details for the letter header. This tells the financial aid office who you are.
🚨
What Happened?
Describe the financial change or hardship clearly and specifically. Be factual and honest.
Be specific about the timing. This helps the aid office understand why your FAFSA may not reflect your current situation.
Writing Tip: Be specific and factual. Instead of "We are having money problems," say "My father was laid off from his position as an electrician at Smith Electric on March 15, 2025, after the company downsized. He has been unable to find comparable employment."
💰
How Does This Affect You?
Explain how this situation impacts your ability to pay for college. Connect the hardship to your educational costs.
Be honest about the gap between what you can now afford and what college costs. Numbers strengthen your case.
Be clear about what you are asking for. You can request a Special Circumstances review, additional grant aid, or a reconsideration of your aid package.
Showing that you are actively addressing the situation demonstrates responsibility and strengthens your appeal.
📋
Supporting Documentation
Check off documents you plan to include. Strong documentation significantly increases your chances of a successful appeal.
Documentation Tip: Financial aid offices need to verify your claims. The more documentation you provide, the faster and more favorably your appeal can be processed. If you do not have a document yet, note that it is "forthcoming" in your letter.
✅
Tone & Final Review
Review your letter preview on the right. Make sure your appeal is professional, respectful, and complete.
Do This
Be factual and specific
Stay respectful and grateful
Include clear numbers/dates
Explain what changed and when
State your request clearly
Express commitment to education
Avoid This
Exaggerating or being dramatic
Blaming the school or system
Being vague about circumstances
Demanding specific dollar amounts
Comparing to other students
Submitting without documentation
Possible Outcomes of Your Appeal
✅Aid Adjustment: Your financial aid eligibility is recalculated based on updated financial information, potentially increasing grants or reducing expected family contribution.
💰Additional Aid: The school may offer additional institutional grants, emergency funds, or work-study opportunities.
📄Updated Package: Your entire financial aid package may be reprocessed using Professional Judgment to reflect your current circumstances.
💬No Change: The office may determine the original package is appropriate. Even so, you may learn about other resources or options available to you.
Before Submitting: 1) Proofread carefully. 2) Have someone else review it. 3) Make copies of everything you submit. 4) Note the date you submit and who you give it to. 5) Follow up in 1-2 weeks if you have not heard back.

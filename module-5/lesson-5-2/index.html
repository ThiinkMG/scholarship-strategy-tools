Your school sets a standard Cost of Attendance (COA) -- but what happens when your actual costs are higher? Learn when and how to request a COA adjustment to unlock more financial aid.

📚 What is Cost of Attendance (COA)?
Your school's Cost of Attendance is the total estimated cost for one year of college. It sets the maximum amount of financial aid you can receive. If your actual expenses exceed the school's standard estimates, you may be able to request a COA adjustment (also called a "budget appeal") to increase your aid eligibility.
